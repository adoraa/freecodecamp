Dr. Dora Akunyili

The woman who fought counterfeit drugs and saved millions of lives

Here's a timeline of Dr. Dora Akunyili's life:

  • 1954 - Born in Makurdi, Benue State, Nigeria.
  • 1966 - Received her First School Leaving Certificate from St. Patrick's Primary School, Isuofia, Anambra State.
  • 1973 - Graduated with Distinction from Queen of the Rosary Secondary School, Nsukka.
  • 1978 - Earned a degree in Pharmacy from the University of Nigeria, Nsukka.
  • 1985 - Received her Ph.D. in Ethnopharmacology from the University of Nigeria, Nsukka.
  • 1988 - Witnessed the tragic death of her sister due to counterfeit insulin, which inspired her fight against fake drugs.
  • 2001 - Appointed Director-General of the National Agency for Food and Drug Administration and Control (NAFDAC).
  • 2003 - Survived an assassination attempt while leading the fight against counterfeit drugs.
  • 2006 - Named one of Time Magazine's "18 Heroes of Our Time."
  • 2008 - Appointed Minister of Information and Communications by President Umaru Yar'Adua.
  • 2010 - Resigned as Minister to run for Senator of Anambra Central, though she was not elected.
  • 2012 - Published her book, The War Against Counterfeit Medicine: My Story.
  • 2014 - Passed away on June 7 after a two-year battle with uterine cancer.

"Dora Akunyili's life and achievements are a testament to the power of courage, integrity, and dedication in the fight for public health and human rights."

-- Former Nigerian President Goodluck Jonathan
